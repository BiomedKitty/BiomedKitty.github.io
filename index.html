About our project

Repetitive Transcranial Stimulation is a non-invasive form of brain stimulation that uses short magnetic bursts to stimulate neurons in the brain. It is currently only used to treat symptoms of major depressive disorder (MDD), obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD), smoking cessation, and various movement disorders (1) . rTMS is becoming more widely accepted as a treatment for neurological and some psychological conditions, however, one of the biggest challenges now is increasing focal precision (2). Our team has been focused on increasing the focality of rtms by using the principles of constructive and destructive interference.
